Our Story

About IICD

The Institute

Who We Are

The International Institute for Creative Development (IICD) is a capacity building centre that provides a sustainable creative database, advocacy, and technical assistance for creating and managing cultural exchange programmes that seek to advance cultural diplomacy and social development.

IICD Center is also a multi-disciplinary experimental art space that inspires a creative environment for open thematic expression within the context of contemporary art practice. Located at 4 Oguda Close, Off Lake Chad Crescent, Maitama, Abuja, the centre serves as a hub for artists, curators, cultural managers, and the wider creative community.

Our Mission

IICD's main objective is to help artists, within the framework of contemporary art practice, establish their voice, connect their paths, and create new narratives. We do this by fostering creative dialogues and interactions between Nigerian artists and resident foreign artists, provoking fresh debates and research opportunities in keeping with worldwide curatorial standards.

Deconstructing the stereotype of Abuja as a soulless metropolis has always been central to our work. Our distinct modus operandi — as an open thematic experimental art space that infuses pedagogical and creative processes into the industry — has repositioned Abuja's art scene as a distinctive cultural identity within Nigeria and internationally.

Our Origins

IICD was founded in 2010 by Nduwhite Ndubuisi Ahanonu and his late friend Harrison Ikibah — a direct offshoot of the Ford Foundation's mentorship programme to develop future cultural managers in Nigeria. The founding came shortly after Ahanonu returned from the 2010 Dakar Biennale, where he represented Nigeria with a fresh perspective on African contemporary art practice.

The pair began by initiating interventions in Abuja's art scene, which was then dominated almost exclusively by government parastatals and embassies. An influential encounter with British Council representatives opened new doors, and Ahanonu went on to study cultural diplomacy in Berlin in 2008, followed by courses in Venice, Dakar, Germany, France, and the United States.

Nduwhite Ndubuisi Ahanonu

Founder & Director

An artist, curator, and art activist born in Imo State, Nigeria. Ahanonu represented Nigeria at the 2010 Dakar Biennale and participated in the Midbar International Artists' Residence in Tel Aviv (2006). His curatorial credentials include "Catalyst for Future Cities" at Transcorp Hilton, the Francophonie Festival performance at the Belgian Ambassador's residence (2017), Abuja Alive (2016), and co-curating the Finding the True North residency in Karlstad, Sweden.

He is an MFA candidate at Leuphana University, Lüneburg, Germany. He has held four solo exhibitions and participated in numerous group shows locally and internationally.

A Decade of Creative Work

2010

IICD Founded

Founded by Nduwhite Ndubuisi Ahanonu and Harrison Ikibah as an offshoot of the Ford Foundation mentorship programme, with a focus on cultural management and contemporary art in Abuja.

2015

Catalyst for Future Cities

Major group exhibition at Transcorp Hilton Hotel, Abuja — "Hilton at 30" — curated by Nduwhite Ndubuisi Ahanonu, marking IICD's growing reach into major institutional venues.

2016

Abuja Alive & Sweden Residency

Curated the Abuja Alive group exhibition at the IICD Centre, and co-curated the Finding the True North artist residency in Karlstad, Sweden — an international exchange between Nigerian and Swedish artists.

2017

Francophonie Festival Performance

"Though I Don't Know You" — a performance held at the Belgian Ambassador's residence in Abuja in celebration of the Francophonie Festival. International residency programme also held in November 2017.

2018

Upcycling: The New Economy & 8th Anniversary

A landmark three-week workshop on upcycling and sustainability, co-organised with the US Embassy, featuring 11 artists from across Nigeria. Portfolio review workshop with France-based curator Caroline Hancock in collaboration with Institut Français. IICD marked eight years with exhibitions and awards to partners.

2019

Inaugural Abuja Open House & 10th Anniversary

The first edition of Abuja's largest art festival, October 25–27 — a four-day event animating creative spaces across the city, created in collaboration with the US Embassy. Coincided with IICD's 10th anniversary.

2022

Abuja Open House 2nd Edition

The second edition of the Open House animated over 22 locations in four days and attracted over 1,500 attendees. Upcycling Redefined workshop also held in collaboration with the US Embassy and Transcorp Hilton.

2023

Abuja Open House 3rd Edition — Catalytic Synthesis

The third edition of the Open House continued to grow the festival's footprint as the most significant visual arts event in Abuja, under the theme Catalytic Synthesis.

2024

Reflection Time is Now

Exhibition at the Atrium Gallery, Foreign Affairs Ministry, Central Business District, Abuja — a programme designed to provoke thought and discussion across cultural boundaries, capturing the Zeitgeist of a generation.
