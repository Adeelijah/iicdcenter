IICD Centre, Maitama

The Gallery

Our Space

A Home for Contemporary Creative Work

The IICD gallery is a dedicated exhibition space in the heart of Maitama, Abuja. Since 2010, it has hosted solo exhibitions, group shows, residency presentations, and collaborative programmes — featuring artists from Nigeria and across the world.

The gallery operates as a white-cube space adaptable to paintings, sculpture, photography, installation, and mixed media. It serves as the primary exhibition venue for IICD's residency programme and is available for hire by artists, curators, and institutions.

Our programming is driven by a commitment to art that has something to say — works that engage with real ideas, provoke conversation, and contribute to the development of the Abuja creative scene as a distinct cultural identity.

Curatorial Vision

Art That Has Something to Say

IICD's gallery programming is guided by the belief that art is more than passive decoration. We curate exhibitions that engage with real ideas — migration, environment, identity, time, and social change — and that create space for conversation across cultural boundaries.

As Chief Curator Nduwhite Ndubuisi Ahanonu has noted, IICD works from the Kandinsky principle: the artist must have something to say. Mastery over form is not the goal — the goal is adapting form to inner meaning.

The gallery has shown artists at all career stages, from PhD scholars and established painters to emerging voices fresh from residency. We are committed to supporting Abuja-based artists and to bringing international perspectives into conversation with local creative practice.

Come to the Gallery
