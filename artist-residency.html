Artist Residency

About the Programme

Space to Create, Connect, and Develop

IICD's international artist residency programme supports emerging and established creatives with dedicated time, space, and a rigorous curatorial framework to develop their practice in Abuja. The programme has, over the years, sparked new creative synergies between Nigerian and international artists.

The residency is designed to provoke fresh debates, arguments, and research opportunities. Residents engage with the local arts community through public events, workshops, and exhibitions, contributing to IICD's broader mission of repositioning the creative narratives of the Abuja art scene.

Past facilitators have included internationally recognised artists, academics, and curators — from Norwegian artist Prof. Vigdis Holen to US Embassy-sponsored sustainability practitioners. The residency typically runs for 30 days and concludes with a public presentation or exhibition of work developed during the period.

"The IICD Centre is a one-of-a-kind space for any growing curator, providing the kind of rigorous learning, development, and kindness that are so important for a budding curator."

— Aisha Aliyu-Bima, Institut Français Curatorial Laureate

Programme Details

What Residents Receive

  • Dedicated studio space at the IICD Centre in Maitama, Abuja
  • Light breakfast at the Centre daily throughout the residency
  • Local travel support for site visits and study trips across Abuja
  • Access to IICD's curated resource library of art books and catalogues
  • Engagement with IICD's network of artists and cultural organisations
  • Mentorship from Founder and Director Nduwhite Ndubuisi Ahanonu
  • Public programme participation — talks, workshops, and open studios
  • Exhibition or public presentation opportunity at the end of residency
  • Accommodation support available for out-of-town participants

Duration & Format

  • Standard residency: 30 days at IICD Centre, Maitama
  • Workshop residencies: 2–3 weeks, topic-focused
  • International exchange residencies: arranged in partnership with overseas institutions
  • Concludes with public sharing, exhibition, or open studio event

Who Should Apply

  • Visual artists, sculptors, photographers, and mixed media practitioners
  • Curators with an upcoming project they wish to develop
  • Architects, designers, and environmentally-focused creatives
  • Open to Nigerian nationals and international applicants
  • Both emerging and established practitioners are welcome
  • Abuja-based Nigerian artists may qualify for a reduced participation fee

How to Apply

  • Submit a letter of intent detailing your proposed project and research
  • Confirm your availability for the full residency duration
  • Include 5 samples of relevant work or project documentation
  • State whether you require accommodation assistance
  • Send to: nduwhite@gmail.com or info@iicd.org
History

Past Residencies

A selection of IICD's residency programmes and the artists and facilitators who have shaped them.

2022

Upcycling Redefined

Three-week workshop in collaboration with the US Embassy, AD Connect, and Transcorp Hilton Abuja. Brought together upcycling artists, architects, engineers, environmentalists, industrial designers, and AI/robotic engineers to re-evaluate their practice and the built environment. 15 creatives selected from an open call.

2018

Upcycling: The New Economy

22 April – 3 May 2018. 11 artists selected from across Nigeria. Facilitated by Dr. Victor Fadeke (Hubert H. Humphrey / Ashoka Fellow), Dr. Chukwuemeka Nwigwe (UNN), Nduwhite Ndubuisi, Ifesinachi C. Nwanyanwu, Dotun Poopola, and Dada A. Oluwasuen. Exhibition of works opened 3 May at IICD Centre.

2018

Portfolio Review Workshop with Caroline Hancock

Organised with France-based curator Caroline Hancock in collaboration with Institut Français, Abuja. Focused on developing portfolio presentation skills for emerging curators and artists — a targeted capacity-building workshop that drew participants from across Nigeria.

2017

International Artists in Residence — November 2017

30-day intensive residency at 4 Oguda Close, Maitama. Artists in residence included Prof. Vigdis Holen (Norway) and others. Concluded with a public sharing session on Saturday 19 November, 3pm, inviting Abuja's public to engage with work developed during the period.

2016

Finding the True North — Karlstad, Sweden

Co-curated international artist residency in Karlstad, Sweden — an exchange bringing Nigerian artists into dialogue with Swedish counterparts. Part of IICD's ongoing commitment to international creative exchange and cultural diplomacy.

Fees

Participation Fees

International Participants

USD 2,500

Covers accommodation for one month at or near the IICD Centre, light daily breakfast, and local travel support for study and site visits. Exact rates vary by residency cycle — contact us for current details and availability.

Nigerian Participants (Abuja-based)

USD 1,000

Nigerian artists who have their own accommodation in Abuja may qualify for the reduced rate. Contact us for current exchange rates, scholarship availability, and subsidised places through our partner organisations.

Some residency cycles are subsidised through partnerships with the US Embassy, Institut Français, and other sponsors. Enquire about scholarship availability and supported places when applying.
