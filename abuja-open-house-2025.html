Abuja Open House Festival 2025: The Interlude

The fifth edition of the Abuja Open House Festival arrived at a moment when many of us needed something often overlooked in a fast-moving world: a pause.

Following a year marked by constant activity, shifting realities, and the demands of everyday life, The Interlude invited participants to step away from the noise and embrace a space for reflection, connection, and renewal. It was conceived as a moment to breathe a deliberate pause between what has been and what is yet to come.

Across exhibitions, conversations, and cultural experiences, the festival explored the relationship between movement and stillness, celebrating the value of slowing down to observe, reflect, and reconnect with ourselves, our communities, and our city.

At the heart of this edition was the recognition that growth is not found only in motion. Sometimes, meaningful transformation happens in moments of rest, contemplation, and quiet observation. Through art and culture, The Interlude encouraged audiences to experience Abuja differently not merely as a city in constant motion, but as a place rich with stories, memories, creativity, and spaces for reflection.

These photographs capture some of the moments, encounters, and experiences that defined this landmark fifth edition. Together, they serve as a visual reminder of a festival that created room for pause, reflection, and shared discovery, demonstrating that even amidst movement, there is beauty in stillness.

About the 2025 Festival

What Happened

  • The fifth edition of the Abuja Open House Festival was presented under the theme "The Interlude"
  • The festival invited participants to pause, reflect, and reconnect amidst the pace of everyday life
  • Exhibitions showcased artistic responses to themes of stillness, contemplation, and renewal
  • Conversations and cultural experiences explored the relationship between movement and stillness
  • Audiences engaged with art, culture, and community through shared experiences across the city
  • Creative programmes encouraged deeper reflection on personal, social, and urban experiences
  • The festival highlighted Abuja as a city rich in stories, memories, creativity, and spaces for reflection
  • Participants experienced new perspectives on the city beyond its constant motion and development
  • Artists, cultural practitioners, and audiences gathered in moments of dialogue and discovery
  • Photographs and documented experiences captured the spirit of pause, connection, and transformation that defined the landmark fifth edition of the festival

The Open House Model

  • Each iteration signifies a point of divergence — discovering, sharing, and learning
  • The festival's unique selling point is its multi-venue model spanning the whole city
  • Guarantees intense art experiences and stimulating civic and cultural exchanges
  • A board of directors leads each edition — new directors chosen and invited each year
  • The mandate: consistently grow and strategically democratise art and culture
  • Aims to take art to communities that are disenfranchised from cultural access
