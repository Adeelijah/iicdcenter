Abuja's Largest Art Festival Returns

The second edition of the Abuja Open House held in 2022 — after its planned 2020 edition was disrupted by the COVID-19 pandemic. Animating over 22 venues across Abuja over four days, it was the most attended art event in the capital city, attracting over 1,500 attendees.

Created and produced by IICD in collaboration with the United States Embassy in Abuja, the Open House is a four-day fiesta conceived to connect and bridge the gap between Abuja's creative audience, art spaces, and galleries — and to democratise access to contemporary art in Nigeria's federal capital.

The 2022 edition attracted an impressive run thanks to its creative director, management team, curators, and — most importantly — volunteers. It recorded a significant increase in gallery visits compared to the 2019 inaugural edition and effectively helped to groom new cultural players in the industry.

About the 2022 Festival

What Happened

  • Multi-venue gallery hops across 22+ locations in Abuja over four days
  • Exhibitions at Fraser Suites and landmark venues across the city
  • Works by artists including John Oyedemi and Akachukwu Chukwuemeka
  • Artist talks, panel discussions, and curatorial conversations
  • Live performances and site-specific interventions
  • VIP receptions and evening private views at major Abuja venues
  • Public screenings and film programmes
  • Workshops and creative sessions open to the public
  • New volunteers trained as cultural industry participants

The Open House Model

  • Each iteration signifies a point of divergence — discovering, sharing, and learning
  • The festival's unique selling point is its multi-venue model spanning the whole city
  • Guarantees intense art experiences and stimulating civic and cultural exchanges
  • A board of directors leads each edition — new directors chosen and invited each year
  • The mandate: consistently grow and strategically democratise art and culture
  • Aims to take art to communities that are disenfranchised from cultural access

The 2023 Edition

  • Third edition held October 2023 under the theme Catalytic Synthesis
  • Continued to grow the festival's footprint as Abuja's signature art event
