Programme History

Over a Decade of Creative Activity

Since 2010, IICD has delivered a rich and varied programme of exhibitions, residencies, workshops, performances, screenings, and public events. This page documents that history — from the inaugural Abuja Open House to landmark solo shows, sustainability workshops, and international exchanges.

Full Programme Record

2025
Festival

Abuja Open House 2025 — The Interlude

Fifth edition. 3 venues, 5 days, 1,500+ attendees. Exhibitions at Fraser Suites and venues across Abuja including works by John Oyedemi and Akachukwu Chukwuemeka. The city's most attended art event.

2024
Exhibition

Reflection Time is Now

Atrium Gallery, Foreign Affairs Ministry, Central Business District, Abuja. Supported by IICD and Mswitch. Opened 3 July 2024. Designed to provoke thought across cultural boundaries and capture the Zeitgeist of a generation.

2023
Festival

Abuja Open House 2023 — Catalytic Synthesis

Third edition. October 2023. Theme: Catalytic Synthesis. Expanded multi-venue programme across Abuja. Described by ThisDay Live as "without a doubt the most significant visual arts festival in the federal capital city."

2022
Festival

Abuja Open House 2022

Second edition. 22+ venues, 4 days, 1,500+ attendees. Exhibitions at Fraser Suites and venues across Abuja including works by John Oyedemi and Akachukwu Chukwuemeka. The city's most attended art event.

2022
Workshop

Upcycling Redefined

Three-week workshop with the US Embassy, AD Connect, and Transcorp Hilton. 15 creatives selected from open call — artists, architects, engineers, environmentalists, industrial designers, and AI/robotic engineers exploring sustainability through creative practice.

2019
Festival

Abuja Open House 2019 — Inaugural Edition

October 25–27, 2019. First edition. Created in collaboration with the US Embassy. Included Time in Perspective group show at Nordic Hotel, Mabushi. Coincided with IICD's 10th anniversary. Awards to US Embassy and Transcorp Hilton.

2019
Solo Exhibition

Bolaji Ogunnwo — Migration

IICD Centre, Abuja. 20 June – 10 July 2019. Painter and University of Lagos lecturer Bolaji Ogunnwo explores global migration challenges facing Nigeria. Over 50 local and international shows to his name.

2018
Solo Exhibition

Prof. Vigdis Holen — Solo Show

IICD Centre, Abuja. Solo exhibition by Norwegian artist Prof. Vigdis Holen, participating in IICD's international residency programme. Part of a sustained series of international artist exchanges at the centre.

2018
Workshop / Exhibition

Upcycling: The New Economy

22 April – 3 May 2018, IICD Centre. 11 artists. Facilitated by Dr. Victor Fadeke (Hubert H. Humphrey / Ashoka Fellow), Dr. Chukwuemeka Nwigwe (UNN), Nduwhite Ndubuisi, Ifesinachi C. Nwanyanwu, Dotun Poopola, Dada A. Oluwasuen. Exhibition opened 3 May, 5pm. US Embassy supported.

2018
Workshop

Portfolio Review with Caroline Hancock

Collaboration with Institut Français, Abuja. France-based curator Caroline Hancock led portfolio review sessions for emerging Nigerian artists and curators — a targeted capacity-building workshop.

2018
Film Screening

Racing Extinction — Film Screening & Discussion

22 April 2018, IICD Centre, 5pm. Oscar-winning documentary directed by Louie Psihoyos. Moderated by Osi Otsemobor in conversation with Romeo Shagba (Video Artist) and Amarachi Okafor (Installation Artist & Curator).

2017
Performance

Though I Don't Know You — Francophonie Festival

Belgian Ambassador's Residence, Abuja. Performance in celebration of the Francophonie Festival. Curated and produced by Nduwhite Ndubuisi Ahanonu. A work exploring cultural encounter and unfamiliarity.

2017
Residency

IICD International Artists in Residence

November 2017. 30-day residency at 4 Oguda Close, Maitama, Abuja. Public sharing event Saturday 19 November, 3pm. International artists in dialogue with the Abuja creative community.

2016
Exhibition

Abuja Alive

Group exhibition at IICD Centre, Abuja. Curated by Nduwhite Ndubuisi Ahanonu. Celebrated the vitality of Abuja's emerging creative community and asserted the city's place in the Nigerian contemporary art landscape.

2016
Residency Exchange

Finding the True North — Karlstad, Sweden

Co-curated international artist residency in Karlstad, Sweden. An exchange between Nigerian and Swedish artists, part of IICD's broader programme of international cultural diplomacy and creative exchange.

2015
Exhibition

Catalyst for Future Cities — Hilton at 30

Transcorp Hilton Hotel, Abuja. Major group exhibition marking the hotel's 30th anniversary. Curated by Nduwhite Ndubuisi Ahanonu. A defining early project for IICD at one of Nigeria's most prestigious venues.

2010
Foundation

IICD Founded

International Institute for Creative Development established by Nduwhite Ndubuisi Ahanonu and Harrison Ikibah as a direct offshoot of the Ford Foundation mentorship programme. Located at Maitama, Abuja, with a mission to advance cultural diplomacy and creative development in Nigeria.
