Our Exhibition Programme

Showcasing Nigerian and African Contemporary Art

IICD curates and hosts a year-round exhibitions programme celebrating the diversity and depth of Nigerian and African creative practice. Our gallery space in Maitama has shown solo exhibitions, group shows, residency outcomes, and collaborative projects with artists from across Nigeria and abroad.

We welcome exhibition proposals from artists and curators. Our programming prioritises works that engage with contemporary issues, cultural exchange, and the evolving identity of the African continent. As Chief Curator Nduwhite Ndubuisi Ahanonu has noted, we operate from the Kandinsky principle: the artist must have something to say.

2019
Group Exhibition · Nordic Hotel, Mabushi, Abuja

Time in Perspective

Presented at the lobby of the Nordic Hotel, Mabushi, Abuja as part of the Abuja Open House 2019. Opened at a VIP reception serenaded by Turkish musical duo Sinan Bora Saribas (violin) and Kutluhan Gozutok (guitar). 2019
Solo Exhibition · IICD Centre, Abuja · 20 June – 10 July 2019

Bolaji Ogunnwo — Migration

Solo exhibition by Lagos-based artist and University of Lagos lecturer Bolaji Ogunnwo, exploring global challenges of migration facing Nigeria. Ogunnwo's work spans over 50 local and international shows. Chief Curator Nduwhite Ndubuisi: "Bolaji is corroborating one of the ideologies of the IICD Center — the artist must have something to say, for mastery over form is not his goal but rather the adapting of form to its inner meaning."

Open Call & Ongoing Programmes

Exhibit at IICD

Ongoing · Open Call

Submit an Exhibition Proposal

IICD welcomes proposals for solo and group exhibitions at the IICD Centre gallery. We are particularly interested in work engaging with cultural exchange, social development, environmental practice, and contemporary African identity. Send a brief proposal and 5 work samples to info@iicd.org.

Annual · Residency Outcome

Residency Exhibition Programme

Each IICD residency cycle concludes with a public exhibition or presentation of work developed at the centre. These shows are open to the public and free to attend. They represent some of the most exciting new work being made in Abuja each year.

Annual · Festival

Abuja Open House Exhibition Programme

Artists can submit work for inclusion in the Abuja Open House multi-venue exhibition programme, held annually across 22+ locations in Abuja over four days. The Open House is the largest platform for visual art in Nigeria's capital.

Year-Round · Curatorial

Curatorial Development Programme

IICD offers structured curatorial classes for emerging curators — learning through real exhibitions, real artists, and real feedback. Participants develop practical curatorial skills through IICD's year-round exhibition programme.
