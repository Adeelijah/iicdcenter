IICD Journal

Reflections on Art, Culture & Creative Practice

Notes, reports, press coverage, and stories from IICD's programmes and the broader Abuja creative scene. This page collects writing about the Open House, residency programme, gallery exhibitions, and IICD's wider cultural mission — in the words of participants, critics, and the IICD team.

Featured · ThisDay Live, November 2024

Meaningful Connections for These Times

Founded in 2010 as a direct offshoot of the Ford Foundation's mentorship programme, IICD has significantly contributed to developing future cultural managers in Nigeria. Recently, it has played a pivotal role behind the scenes in positioning Abuja's art scene as a distinct cultural identity.

"Abuja may appear soulless from afar, as many have described it in the past. However, deconstructing this psychological stereotype is part of the IICD Center's mission."

The profile traces IICD's journey from its founding by Nduwhite Ndubuisi Ahanonu and the late Harrison Ikibah, through its growing programme of residencies, exhibitions, and the Abuja Open House festival — the largest visual arts event in Nigeria's federal capital city.

Open House · 2023

In Abuja, a Fiesta Conceived to Connect the Dots

The third edition of the Abuja Open House — themed Catalytic Synthesis — is a one-of-a-kind blend of dynamic art experiences and gallery hopping that provides solid foundations for convergence of Abuja's creative framework: discovering, sharing, and learning before merging into what co-founder Nduwhite Ahanonu calls "a triangle." Over 22 venues, four days, an unmissable event in the Nigerian capital. Its unique selling proposition: guaranteeing intense art and creative experiences, stimulating discussions, and assured civic and cultural exchanges.

ThisDay Live · October 2023
Curatorial · Profile

Charting a New Course for the Abuja Art Scene

With lustrous curatorial credentials, artist Nduwhite Ndubuisi Ahanonu has initiated landmark projects to dispel Abuja's unflattering reputation as a soulless metropolis. From the Dakar Biennale to Berlin, Venice, and Karlstad — and back to Maitama. His IICD Center, established in 2010, focuses on developing creative talent and managing cultural exchange programmes. "Abuja is a city with enormous creative potential. Our job is to make that visible."

ThisDay Live · March 2023
Residency · 2022

Upcycling Redefined: Art, Architecture & the Circular Economy

For three weeks, 15 creatives — artists, architects, engineers, AI/robotic engineers, and environmental practitioners — came together at IICD for the Upcycling Redefined workshop, supported by the US Embassy, AD Connect, and Transcorp Hilton. The result was a new body of work and a deepened conversation about sustainability as creative practice. "Life seeks more of itself, wastes nothing and engages fruitfully with its surroundings."

IICD · 2022
Mentorship · Curating

What IICD Taught Me About Curating

"Its founder, Nduwhite Ndubuisi Ahanonu, provided me with such direct mentorship in a space where I am constantly learning and putting ideas into practice. The IICD curatorial classes have been of immense benefit, adding technical discipline to everything I do. I think the IICD Centre is a one-of-a-kind space for any growing curator — providing the kind of rigorous learning, development, and kindness that are so important for a budding curator in Nigeria."

Aisha Aliyu-Bima, Institut Français Curatorial Laureate · 2023
Exhibition · 2019

Migration, Memory, and the Art of Bolaji Ogunnwo

Lagos-based painter and University of Lagos lecturer Bolaji Ogunnwo brought his solo exhibition on global migration challenges to the IICD Centre in June 2019. Chief Curator Nduwhite Ndubuisi noted: "Bolaji is corroborating one of the ideologies of the IICD Center — the artist must have something to say, for mastery over form is not his goal but rather the adapting of form to its inner meaning." Ogunnwo's work spans over 50 local and international shows.

IICD · June 2019
Workshop · 2018

Upcycling: The New Economy — A Report

Eleven artists from across Nigeria gathered at IICD in April 2018 for a landmark workshop on upcycling and sustainability, supported by the US Embassy. Facilitated by Dr. Victor Fadeke (Hubert H. Humphrey / Ashoka Fellow), Dr. Chukwuemeka Nwigwe, and others. The opening of works drew Abuja's creative community to the IICD Centre on 3 May. A rich conversation between art, ecology, and the future of the Nigerian creative economy.

IICD · May 2018
